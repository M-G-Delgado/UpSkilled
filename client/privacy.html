Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 27, 2025

This Privacy Policy outlines how UpSkilled GmbH collects, processes, and protects personal data, ensuring compliance with GDPR, BDSG, and other applicable German laws. It applies to all Users and Providers interacting with our Platform.

1. Data Controller and Contact Details

The data controller under Art. 4(7) GDPR is:

UpSkilled GmbH

60313 Frankfurt am Main, Germany
Email: privacy@upskilled.de
Phone: +49 69 12345678

Our Data Protection Officer (DPO) is reachable at dpo@upskilled.de. For inquiries or concerns, contact us directly.

2. Definitions

  • Platform: UpSkilled website, mobile app (if applicable), and related services.
  • User: Any individual or entity accessing the Platform.
  • Learner: Users booking or attending workshops.
  • Provider: Businesses or instructors offering workshops.
  • Personal Data: Information relating to an identified or identifiable person (Art. 4(1) GDPR).

3. General Principles of Data Processing

We process personal data lawfully, fairly, and transparently (Art. 5 GDPR). Processing occurs only with legal bases:

  • Consent: Your explicit agreement (Art. 6(1)(a)).
  • Contract: To fulfill bookings or services (Art. 6(1)(b)).
  • Legitimate Interest: For Platform functionality, security, and improvement (Art. 6(1)(f)), balanced against your rights.
  • Legal Obligation: To comply with German law (e.g., tax retention, § 147 AO) (Art. 6(1)(c)).

Data is minimized to what is necessary and stored only as long as required.

4. Data Collection and Purposes

4.1 Website Usage

When you visit the Platform, we automatically collect:

  • IP address (anonymized after 24 hours)
  • Date, time, and duration of access
  • Browser type, version, and settings
  • Operating system
  • Referring URL and pages visited
  • Device identifiers (e.g., hashed MAC address)

Purpose: Ensure functionality, troubleshoot errors, and enhance security (Art. 6(1)(f)). Logs are retained for 7 days unless needed for legal defense (§ 76 BDSG).

4.2 Account Registration and Booking

For accounts or bookings, we collect:

  • Name
  • Email address
  • Password (hashed)
  • City (optional)
  • Payment details (processed by third parties)
  • Skill Points and Levels (gamification data)
  • Booking history

Purpose: Account management, contract fulfillment, and personalized services (Art. 6(1)(b)). Providers receive name, email, and booking details only for workshop execution.

4.3 Newsletter and Marketing

For newsletters, we collect:

  • Email address
  • City (optional)
  • Subscription preferences

Purpose: Direct marketing with consent (Art. 6(1)(a)). Unsubscribe via email links or privacy@upskilled.de.

4.4 Provider Data

For Providers, we collect:

  • Business name
  • Contact person’s name, email, and phone
  • Bank details (for commission payments)
  • Workshop descriptions and schedules
  • Tax identification number (§ 14 UStG)

Purpose: Partnership management and payment processing (Art. 6(1)(b)). Providers must ensure their data accuracy; errors are their responsibility.

4.5 Special Cases

  • Minors: Parental consent data (e.g., guardian name) for Users under 18 (§ 8 GDPR, § 2 JuSchG).
  • Subsidized Learners: Verification data (e.g., income proof) for underserved groups (Art. 6(1)(c)).
  • Disability Accommodations: Accessibility requests (e.g., mobility needs) shared with Providers (Art. 9(2)(a)).
  • Disputes: Communication records for legal defense (Art. 6(1)(f)).

5. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use:

  • Essential Cookies: For session management and security (Art. 6(1)(f)).
  • Analytics Cookies: For usage statistics (e.g., Google Analytics) with consent (Art. 6(1)(a)).
  • Marketing Cookies: For targeted ads with consent (Art. 6(1)(a)).

Manage preferences via our consent banner or browser settings (§ 25 TTDSG). Providers may not deploy additional trackers without our approval.

6. Data Recipients

We share data with:

  • Hosting Providers: [Provider Name], Germany, for server operations (Art. 28 GDPR).
  • Payment Processors: Stripe, EU-based, for transactions (Art. 6(1)(b)).
  • Providers: Name, email, and booking details for workshop delivery (Art. 6(1)(b)).
  • Analytics Services: Google Analytics, with IP anonymization (Art. 6(1)(a)).
  • Legal Authorities: If required by law (e.g., § 24 BDSG).

Providers are contractually bound as processors (Art. 28 GDPR) and liable for misuse. No transfers outside the EU/EEA occur without safeguards (e.g., Standard Contractual Clauses, Art. 46 GDPR).

7. Data Retention

Data is retained as follows:

  • Website Logs: 7 days, extendable for security investigations (§ 76 BDSG).
  • Account Data: Until deletion or 3 years after inactivity (§ 195 BGB).
  • Booking Data: 10 years for tax purposes (§ 147 AO).
  • Newsletter Data: Until unsubscribed.
  • Provider Data: Until partnership ends, plus 10 years for tax records.
  • Dispute Data: Until resolution, plus 3 years (§ 195 BGB).

Data is deleted or anonymized post-retention unless legally required otherwise.

8. Your Rights

Under GDPR and BDSG, you have the right to:

  • Access your data (Art. 15)
  • Rectify inaccuracies (Art. 16)
  • Erase data ("right to be forgotten," Art. 17)
  • Restrict processing (Art. 18)
  • Data portability (Art. 20)
  • Object to processing (Art. 21)
  • Withdraw consent (Art. 7(3))
  • Not be subject to automated decisions impacting you significantly (Art. 22)

Exercise rights by contacting privacy@upskilled.de. Complaints can be filed with the Hessische Beauftragte für Datenschutz und Informationsfreiheit (datenschutz.hessen.de). Providers have similar rights for their data.

9. Data Security

We implement:

  • SSL/TLS encryption (Art. 32 GDPR).
  • Access controls and regular audits.
  • pseudonymization where feasible.

We are not liable for breaches due to User or Provider negligence (e.g., weak passwords, § 254 BGB). Providers must secure Learner data independently; breaches are their responsibility.

10. Special Situations and Protections

10.1 Minors and Vulnerable Groups

Data from minors (under 18) or vulnerable adults (e.g., under guardianship, § 1896 BGB) is processed with consent from guardians (Art. 8 GDPR). We are not liable for unauthorized access by minors (§ 832 BGB applies to guardians).

10.2 Providers and Data Sharing

Providers receive minimal Learner data (name, email, booking details) and must not misuse it (e.g., for unsolicited marketing). Violations indemnify us for fines or damages (Art. 82 GDPR).

10.3 Data Breaches

We notify affected Users and authorities within 72 hours of a breach (Art. 33 GDPR) unless risk is low. We are not liable for breaches caused by Providers or third-party processors beyond our control.

10.4 Legal Requests

Data may be disclosed to authorities under § 24 BDSG or court orders (§ 242 ZPO). We are not liable for compliance-related disclosures.

10.5 International Use

Users or Providers outside Germany assume risks of local law conflicts; we are not liable for non-EU data issues unless we initiate transfers.

10.6 Force Majeure

No liability for data loss from uncontrollable events (e.g., cyberattacks, natural disasters) (§ 275 BGB).

11. Third-Party Services

We use:

  • Google Analytics: With IP anonymization, consent-based (Art. 6(1)(a)). Opt-out via banner.
  • Stripe: Payment processing, EU-based (Art. 6(1)(b)).
  • Social Media Plugins: Only with consent (Art. 6(1)(a)).

We are not liable for third-party data practices beyond our processor agreements (Art. 28 GDPR).

12. Business Partner Responsibilities

Providers must:

  • Comply with GDPR/BDSG as joint controllers where applicable (Art. 26 GDPR).
  • Not process Learner data beyond workshop needs without consent.
  • Indemnify us for breaches (e.g., data leaks, fines) (§ 280 BGB).
  • Secure their systems; we are not liable for their failures.

We may audit Providers for compliance, terminating partnerships for violations without liability.

13. Changes to This Policy

Updates will be posted here and notified via email for significant changes. Continued use post-update implies consent (Art. 7 GDPR). Providers are notified separately.
